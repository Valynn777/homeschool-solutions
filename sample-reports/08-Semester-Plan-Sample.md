## Semester Overview

This semester plan provides a **roadmap for steady progress** across all subjects while maintaining flexibility for your family's unique rhythm. The plan accounts for holidays, feast days, co-op breaks, and real life.

**Semester Theme:** *Building Strong Foundations*
This semester, we're focusing on establishing consistent habits, deepening core skills, and nurturing a love for learning rooted in faith.

## Subject Goals by End of Semester

### Mathematics
**Goal:** Complete Teaching Textbooks 5, Lessons 1-75 (approximately half the program)

**Milestones:**
- **By End of February:** Lessons 1-20 (place value, addition/subtraction review, intro to multiplication)
- **By End of March:** Lessons 21-40 (multiplication/division practice, fractions review)
- **By End of April:** Lessons 41-60 (decimals intro, word problems, measurement)
- **By End of May:** Lessons 61-75 (geometry basics, area/perimeter, review)

**Weekly Pace:** 3-4 lessons per week (slower is fine—mastery over speed!)

**Success Indicators:**
- Noah can recall 6-9 multiplication facts with confidence
- Completes lessons with 80%+ accuracy
- Growing independence in following lesson instructions
- Fewer careless errors (evidence of self-checking)

---

### Language Arts

#### Reading
**Goal:** Read 15-20 high-quality books independently + complete 3-4 read-aloud chapter books as a family

**Book List (Suggested):**
- **Independent Reading:** Selections from adventure, historical fiction, nature, and biography
- **Read-Aloud:** *The Hiding Place*, *Carry On, Mr. Bowditch*, *The Bronze Bow*, and one additional title of choice

**Success Indicators:**
- Noah reads daily for pleasure
- Improved fluency and expression when reading aloud
- Demonstrates comprehension through oral narration
- Growing vocabulary

---

#### Writing & Composition
**Goal:** Complete IEW Level A, Units 1-4 (Intro to structure, summarizing narrative, retelling historical narrative, summarizing references)

**Milestones:**
- **February:** Unit 1 (Key Word Outlines, oral narration)
- **March:** Unit 2 (Written narration from outlines, basic structure)
- **April:** Unit 3 (Adding details, stronger sentences)
- **May:** Unit 4 (Summarizing from multiple sources, simple research)

**Success Indicators:**
- Noah can create a simple outline from a reading
- Writes 3-5 sentence paragraphs with clear topic sentence
- Growing stamina for written work (less resistance)
- Understands basic structure (introduction, body, conclusion)

---

#### Grammar
**Goal:** Complete Simply Grammar Level 1, Lessons 1-60

**Weekly Pace:** 3-4 lessons per week (short, oral-focused lessons)

**Success Indicators:**
- Identifies parts of speech (noun, verb, adjective, adverb)
- Understands sentence structure (subject/predicate)
- Can correctly capitalize proper nouns and sentence beginnings
- Applies basic punctuation (periods, question marks, commas in lists)

---

### Science
**Goal:** Complete Elemental Science Biology, Weeks 1-18 (Introduction to living things, plants, simple animals)

**Weekly Pace:** 2 lessons per week

**Milestones:**
- **February:** Intro to biology, characteristics of life, classification
- **March:** Plant structure, photosynthesis, life cycles
- **April:** Simple animals (insects, worms, mollusks)
- **May:** Review and culminating project (nature journal or display board)

**Success Indicators:**
- Noah completes experiments and activities with enthusiasm
- Can narrate key concepts from readings
- Science notebook shows observations and drawings
- Demonstrates curiosity and asks scientific questions

**Milestones:**
- **February:** Lessons 1-6 (Early Church, Roman Empire decline)
- **March:** Lessons 7-12 (Byzantium, Islam, Dark Ages)

## Assessment & Progress Tracking

### Monthly Check-Ins
At the end of each month, review:
- Are we on pace with curriculum goals?
- Is Noah making progress in areas of need (focus, independence, confidence)?
- Are we maintaining joy and peace in our homeschool?
- Do we need to adjust pacing or approach?

### End-of-Semester Evaluation
At the end of May, celebrate and assess:
- Academic progress in each subject
- Growth in character and learning habits
- Favorite books, projects, and memories
- Goals for next semester

---

## Flexible Adjustments

### If You're Ahead of Schedule:
- Add enrichment (extra read-alouds, art projects, field trips)
- Dive deeper into topics of interest
- Start next curriculum unit early

### If You're Behind Schedule:
- Evaluate: Is this curriculum too challenging? Too much volume? Life circumstances?
- Adjust: Drop one content subject temporarily, slow pacing, combine subjects
- Remember: Progress over perfection!

---

## Parent Self-Care & Sustainability

Homeschooling is a marathon, not a sprint. Build in:
- **Weekly Sabbath rest** — truly rest on Shabbat
- **Monthly encouragement** — Connect with other homeschool moms, read an encouraging book
- **Grace for hard days** — Some days you'll just survive, and that's okay
- **Celebration of wins** — Notice and celebrate Noah's growth and your faithfulness
