## Reading & Language Arts

### Writing

**Assessment Method:** Writing samples, dictation, oral composition

**Strengths:**
- Good ideas and creative thinking
- Strong oral composition—can narrate clearly and thoughtfully
- Improving sentence structure
- Growing understanding of paragraph organization

**Growth Areas:**

- Asks excellent scientific questions
- Enjoys experiments and hands-on exploration
- Good observational skills

**Growth Areas:**

- Needs to develop scientific vocabulary

## History & Geography

### Current Level
**History Knowledge:** Early Elementary (gaps due to informal approach)
**Geography Skills:** Developing

**Strengths:**
- Enjoys stories and narratives from history
- Remembers details from historical fiction and read-alouds
- Interested in how people lived in the past

**Growth Areas:**
- Limited chronological framework—unclear timeline of major events
- Needs more exposure to world geography and map skills
- Could benefit from hands-on projects or timeline work

**Assessment Notes:**
Noah has been exposed to history sporadically through Story of the World and historical fiction. He enjoys the subject but lacks a structured foundation.

**Recommendation:** Adopt a consistent history program with read-alouds, narration, timeline, and map work. Story of the World or The Mystery of History are both excellent choices. Aim for 2-3 times per week. Add a map skills component and simple timeline to build spatial and chronological understanding.
