Ancient Egypt
CIVILIZATION OF THE NILE

The Gift of the Nile

Ancient Egypt was one of the world's greatest civilizations, flourishing from around 2500 BC to 30 BC—beginning several centuries after the Tower of Babel. The mighty Nile River made life possible in the desert by providing water, fertile soil, and transportation.

Egyptian farmers grew crops like wheat and barley in the rich black soil left behind by annual floods. This abundance of food allowed the civilization to thrive and develop remarkable achievements in art, architecture, and science.

AMAZING ACHIEVEMENTS
  • The Great Pyramids were built as tombs for pharaohs and took decades to construct using millions of stone blocks.
  • Egyptians developed one of the first writing systems called hieroglyphics, using pictures and symbols to record their history.
  • They created a 365-day calendar based on the cycles of the sun and the Nile's flooding patterns.
  • Egyptian doctors were skilled in medicine and performed surgeries, set broken bones, and created remedies from plants.
  • They believed in life after death and developed mummification to preserve bodies for the afterlife.
