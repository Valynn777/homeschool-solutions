The Water Cycle

Water is constantly moving on, above, and below the surface of the Earth. This continuous movement is called the water cycle or hydrologic cycle.

The sun heats up water in rivers, lakes, and oceans, turning it into water vapor through a process called evaporation. Water vapor rises into the air and cools down, forming tiny droplets that create clouds through condensation.

When clouds become heavy with water droplets, precipitation occurs in the form of rain, snow, sleet, or hail. This water falls back to Earth, where it collects in bodies of water or soaks into the ground. The cycle then begins again!
